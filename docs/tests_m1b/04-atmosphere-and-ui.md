# Test Guide: Weather, Descriptions & Shaders

Covers **Steps 13 (Weather & Atmosphere), 15 (Location Descriptions), 16 (Shader Compilation)**.

---

## Step 13 — Weather & Atmosphere

Weather effects change the visual atmosphere of the game world.

### Test Procedure

1. Launch any scenario (e.g., `--scenario terrain_test` or `generated`)
2. Play for a few minutes — weather may cycle naturally
3. Observe visual changes as weather transitions

### Weather Effects to Verify

| # | Weather | Visual Check |
|---|---------|-------------|
| 1 | Clear | Blue sky, full sunlight, sharp shadows |
| 2 | Rain | Particle rain falling, terrain appears wet/darker, splashes on water surfaces |
| 3 | Snow | White particles falling, terrain gradually turns white with snow accumulation |
| 4 | Dust storm | Yellow/brown haze, reduced visibility, particles blowing horizontally |
| 5 | Radiation fog | Green-tinted fog, eerie glow, reduced visibility with a toxic feel |

### Day/Night Cycle

| # | Check | Expected |
|---|-------|----------|
| 1 | Dawn | Orange/pink sky gradient, soft warm lighting |
| 2 | Daytime | Bright white/blue lighting, clear shadows |
| 3 | Dusk | Orange/purple sky, lengthening shadows |
| 4 | Night | Dark blue sky, moonlight, reduced visibility |
| 5 | Smooth transitions | Lighting changes gradually, no sudden pops |

## Step 15 — Location Descriptions

Points of interest show atmospheric descriptions generated by LLM with template fallbacks.

### Test Procedure

1. Launch with `--scenario generated` or `town`
2. Walk near a point of interest (town, building, landmark)
3. Press **N** to open the info panel

### What to Verify

| # | Check | Expected |
|---|-------|----------|
| 1 | Panel appears | Info panel slides in from the right side of the screen |
| 2 | Tagline | A short 1-line atmospheric summary of the location |
| 3 | Expand to summary | Click "Read more" or interact — a paragraph-length description appears |
| 4 | Full description | Click "Full description" — multi-paragraph text with sensory details |
| 5 | Close panel | Press **N** again or click close — panel slides out |
| 6 | Caching | Open the same location again — description loads instantly (cached in SQLite) |
| 7 | Different locations | Visit multiple POIs — each has unique descriptions matching its type/biome |

### Description Quality

Good descriptions should include:
- Atmospheric details matching the post-apocalyptic theme
- References to the location's function (hospital, school, factory, etc.)
- Environmental cues (radiation, rust, decay, nature reclaiming)
- Consistent tone across the three levels (tagline → summary → dossier)

## Step 16 — Shader Compilation

Custom SDSL shaders enhance liquid and terrain rendering with animated effects.

### Test Procedure

1. Build the solution: `dotnet build Oravey2.sln`
2. Verify shaders are in output:

```powershell
Get-ChildItem src/Oravey2.Windows/bin/Debug/net10.0/Effects/*.sdsl
```

**Expected files:** `WaterShader.sdsl`, `LavaShader.sdsl`, `ToxicShader.sdsl`, `OilShader.sdsl`, `FrozenShader.sdsl`, `AnomalyShader.sdsl`, `TerrainSplatShader.sdsl`

3. Launch `--scenario terrain_test`

### What to Verify

| # | Check | Expected |
|---|-------|----------|
| 1 | Water animation | Water surfaces show animated waves/ripples, not flat blue |
| 2 | Lava glow | Lava pools show glowing orange with dark cooling crust |
| 3 | Toxic pulsing | Toxic puddles pulse with a sickly green glow |
| 4 | Terrain blending | Terrain surfaces blend smoothly between different types (splatmap) |
| 5 | No shader errors | Game log contains no "shader compilation failed" errors |
| 6 | Fallback materials | If shaders fail to compile at runtime, liquids still render with flat colored fallback materials |

### Shader → Liquid Mapping

| Liquid Type | Shader | Visual |
|-------------|--------|--------|
| Water | WaterShader | Blue with waves and transparency |
| Toxic / Acid | ToxicShader | Green with pulsing glow |
| Sewage | WaterShader | Murky brown water variant |
| Lava | LavaShader | Orange glow with dark crust |
| Oil | OilShader | Dark glossy surface |
| Frozen | FrozenShader | Ice-blue translucent surface |
| Anomaly | AnomalyShader | Distorted, otherworldly effect |

## Combined Test: Full Exploration

This exercise tests Steps 13, 15, and 16 together:

1. Start a generated world (`--scenario generated`)
2. Walk to a town and press **N** to read about it (Step 15)
3. Close the panel and walk to a nearby water feature (Step 16)
4. Observe liquid shader effects — water should animate
5. Wait for a weather change or play through a day/night cycle (Step 13)
6. Notice how weather affects the visual mood of liquid surfaces
7. Visit another location and check its description is different from the first
